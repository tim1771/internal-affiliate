Affiliate Marketing Glossary

Complete Dictionary of Terms You Need to Know

A

Affiliate

A person or company that promotes products or services for a merchant in exchange for a commission on sales or leads generated through their marketing efforts.

Affiliate Agreement

The legal contract between an affiliate and a merchant/network that outlines terms, commission rates, payment schedules, and rules for promotion.

Affiliate Link

A unique URL containing tracking parameters that identifies which affiliate referred a visitor. When someone clicks and makes a purchase, the affiliate receives credit.

Affiliate Network

A platform that connects merchants with affiliates, handles tracking, and manages payments. Examples: ShareASale, CJ Affiliate, Impact.

Attribution

The process of determining which affiliate gets credit for a conversion. Common models include "last click" (final affiliate before purchase gets credit) and "first click."

AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)

Optimizing content to be cited by AI-powered search engines and chatbots like ChatGPT, Google SGE, and Perplexity.

B

Banner Ad

Graphical advertisements provided by merchants for affiliates to display on their websites. Clicking leads to the merchant's site via the affiliate link.

Bounce Rate

The percentage of visitors who leave a website after viewing only one page. Lower bounce rates generally indicate better engagement.

Bridge Page

A landing page between traffic source and affiliate offer that pre-sells the visitor, collects emails, or warms up the lead before redirecting.

Buyer Intent

The likelihood that a searcher is ready to make a purchase. Keywords like "best [product] for" or "[product] review" indicate high buyer intent.

C

Call-to-Action (CTA)

A prompt encouraging the user to take a specific action, such as "Buy Now," "Check Price," or "Start Free Trial."

Chargeback

When a customer disputes a purchase and the sale is reversed, affiliates typically lose the commission for that sale.

Click-Through Rate (CTR)

The percentage of people who click on a link compared to the total number who viewed it. CTR = (Clicks / Impressions) x 100.

Cloaking

Using a redirect to transform long, ugly affiliate URLs into shorter, cleaner links. Example: yourdomain.com/recommends/product instead of merchant.com/?ref=123abc...

Commission

The payment an affiliate receives for generating a sale, lead, or other desired action. Can be a percentage of sale or flat fee.

Conversion

When a visitor completes a desired action (purchase, signup, download) that earns the affiliate a commission.

Conversion Rate

The percentage of visitors who complete a conversion. Conversion Rate = (Conversions / Total Visitors) x 100.

Cookie

A small file stored in a user's browser that tracks which affiliate referred them. Essential for affiliate attribution.

Cookie Duration

How long the tracking cookie remains active after a click. If a user purchases within this window, the affiliate gets credit. Ranges from 24 hours to 90+ days.

CPA (Cost Per Action)

Commission model where affiliates earn for specific actions like form submissions, signups, or downloads, not just sales.

CPC (Cost Per Click)

A model where affiliates earn money for each click generated, regardless of whether a sale occurs. Rare in affiliate marketing.

CPL (Cost Per Lead)

Commission model where affiliates earn for generating qualified leads (e.g., email signups, quote requests).

CPS (Cost Per Sale)

The most common commission model - affiliates earn a percentage or flat fee when a referred visitor makes a purchase.

D

Deep Link

An affiliate link that points to a specific product page rather than the merchant's homepage. Generally converts better than generic homepage links.

Disclosure

A statement informing readers that you may earn commission from affiliate links. Required by FTC in the US and equivalent bodies in other countries.

E

EPC (Earnings Per Click)

Average earnings generated per 100 clicks. A key metric for comparing program profitability. Calculated as (Total Earnings / Total Clicks) x 100.

Evergreen Content

Content that remains relevant and useful over time, continuing to attract traffic and generate revenue for months or years.

F

FTC (Federal Trade Commission)

US government agency that regulates advertising, including requirements for affiliate disclosure. Non-compliance can result in significant fines.

I

Impression

Each time an ad or affiliate link is displayed to a user, regardless of whether they click.

L

Landing Page

The first page a visitor sees after clicking a link. Can be your content page or the merchant's sales page.

Lead Magnet

A free resource (ebook, checklist, guide) offered in exchange for an email address to build your subscriber list.

Link Building

The process of acquiring backlinks from other websites to improve SEO and domain authority.

M

Merchant

The company that sells products or services and pays affiliates for referrals. Also called advertiser, brand, or vendor.

Micro-Niche

A highly specific, narrow segment within a broader market. Example: "Intermittent fasting for women over 40" within the health niche.

N

Niche

A specialized segment of the market with specific needs and interests. The topic area where you focus your affiliate marketing efforts.

O

Organic Traffic

Visitors who arrive at your site through unpaid search engine results, as opposed to paid advertising.

P

Payout Threshold

The minimum amount of earnings required before an affiliate network will process your payment. Typically $25-$100.

Pixel

A tracking code placed on a merchant's site to record conversions and attribute them to the correct affiliate.

Publisher

Another term for affiliate - the person or company that publishes content promoting merchant products.

R

Recurring Commission

Commission paid repeatedly for as long as a referred customer remains subscribed to a service. Common with SaaS products.

Referral

A visitor sent to a merchant's site through an affiliate link.

RevShare (Revenue Share)

A commission model where affiliates earn a percentage of ongoing revenue from referred customers, not just the initial sale.

ROI (Return on Investment)

The profit earned relative to the investment made. ROI = (Profit - Cost) / Cost x 100.

S

SEO (Search Engine Optimization)

The practice of optimizing content to rank higher in search engine results, driving organic traffic.

SERP (Search Engine Results Page)

The page displayed by search engines in response to a query, where you want your content to rank.

Sub-ID

Additional tracking parameters added to affiliate links to identify which specific content, page, or campaign generated a conversion.

T

Tracking

The technology that records clicks, conversions, and sales, attributing them to the correct affiliate for commission payment.

Two-Tier Program

An affiliate program that pays commission not only on your referrals but also on sales made by affiliates you recruit.

U

UTM Parameters

Tags added to URLs to track the source, medium, and campaign of traffic in analytics tools.

W

White Label

Products or services produced by one company that others rebrand and sell as their own.
