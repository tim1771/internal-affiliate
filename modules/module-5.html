📖 Affiliate Networks Guide

Networks vs. Direct Programs

There are two main ways to get affiliate links: through networks or directly from merchants.

Affiliate Networks Direct Programs
One platform, many merchants Apply directly to each brand
Centralized dashboard and payments Separate logins for each program
Standardized tracking and reporting Varying tracking systems
May have lower commission rates Often higher commissions (no middleman)
Examples: ShareASale, CJ, Impact Examples: Amazon Associates, Shopify Affiliates

Best Practice

Use a mix of both. Networks are great for discovering programs and consolidated reporting. Direct programs often offer better terms and closer merchant relationships. Start with networks to learn the ropes, then add direct programs for your top-performing products.

Reading Affiliate Agreement Terms

Before joining any program, understand these key terms:

Commission Rate

What percentage or flat fee you earn per sale. Compare programs in your niche - rates vary wildly from 1% to 75%.

Cookie Duration

How long after a click you'll get credit for a sale. Longer is better: 24 hours (Amazon) vs 90 days (some SaaS) makes a huge difference.

Payment Threshold

Minimum earnings before you can withdraw. Typical: $25-100. Lower thresholds mean faster access to your money.

Payment Frequency

How often you get paid. Monthly is standard, some offer weekly. Also check payment delay (e.g., "Net 30" = paid 30 days after month end).

Attribution Model

"Last click" gives credit to the final affiliate link clicked. Some programs use "first click" or multi-touch attribution.

Prohibited Methods

What you can't do: bidding on brand keywords, email marketing, incentivized clicks, etc. Violations = account termination.

How to Find Programs in Your Niche

1

Check Product Websites

Scroll to the footer of any product website. Look for links labeled "Affiliates," "Partners," "Refer a Friend," or "Earn with Us." This is often the highest-paying option.

2

Google Search

Search: "[Product Name] affiliate program" or "[Brand] partner program". Also try "[Niche] affiliate programs" for lists.

3

Browse Network Directories

Sign up for ShareASale, Impact, and CJ. Browse their merchant directories by category. Filter by your niche to discover programs you didn't know existed.

4

Study Competitors

Check what programs successful sites in your niche promote. Use browser extensions like "Similar Web" or just look at their disclosure pages.

5

Ask Directly

If a product doesn't list an affiliate program, email them! Many brands have programs they don't advertise, or will create custom arrangements for the right partners.

Getting Approved: Application Tips

What Programs Look For

Most programs want to see:

1. Relevant Content: Your site/channel should be related to their product category
2. Quality: Professional appearance, original content, no spam
3. Traffic: Some programs require minimum traffic; others don't
4. Compliance: Evidence you understand FTC disclosure requirements

Application Best Practices

  • ✓
    Have content published first: Don't apply with an empty site. Have at least 5-10 quality posts or videos in your niche before applying to serious programs.
  • ✓
    Complete your profile: Fill out all fields. Include your website URL, traffic estimates (even if low), and content strategy.
  • ✓
    Explain your promotional plans: Be specific: "I'll create product reviews, comparison guides, and tutorial content" is better than "I'll promote on social media."
  • ✓
    Mention your audience: Describe who visits your site and why this product would help them. Show you understand their customer.
  • ✓
    Use a professional email: yourname@yourdomain.com looks better than randomnumbers@gmail.com.
  • ✓
    Don't be discouraged by rejections: Some programs reject initially but approve later. Apply again after building more content, or try similar programs.

Managing Your Affiliate Links

Once approved, you'll need to manage potentially hundreds of links. Here's how:

Link Management Plugins (WordPress)

  • ThirstyAffiliates: Cloaks links (yourdomain.com/recommends/product), tracks clicks, auto-links keywords
  • Pretty Links: Similar functionality, clean interface
  • Lasso: Premium option with product displays and automated link checking

Why Use Link Management?

  • Clean URLs: Replace ugly affiliate URLs with branded links
  • Easy Updates: Change links in one place, updates everywhere
  • Click Tracking: See which links perform best
  • Broken Link Alerts: Know when programs end or links break
